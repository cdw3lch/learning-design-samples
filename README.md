# Interactive Math & Learning Design — Samples

By **Christopher Welch** — mathematics educator and learning designer (M.Ed. Education Technology & Instructional Design; 13 years teaching and examining).

**▶ Live site:** https://cdw3lch.github.io/learning-design-samples/

A single, self-contained page (no libraries) showing how I introduce a new concept — start from what the learner already knows, make it something they can move and see, and let the formal result fall out of their own reasoning.

### What's inside
- **Interactive math explainers** — completing the square (geometric), why a negative times a negative is positive (pattern + proof), the derivative from first principles (secant → tangent), and a visual proof that the sum of the first *n* odd numbers is *n*².
- **A learning-design case study** — a self-paced online course I designed end to end (concept pages, reflections, and assessment with feedback), shown as a truncated, clickable mockup plus the broader course map.
- **Built accessible** — keyboard-navigable, semantic headings, visible focus, text descriptions for every visual, live-updating explanations for screen readers, and reduced-motion support.
