# Interactive Math & Learning Design — Samples

By **Christopher Welch** — mathematics educator and learning designer. M.Ed. Education Technology & Instructional Design; PGCE Secondary Mathematics & QTS (Hons); B.Sc. Economics (Hons); IGCSE Further Pure Mathematics examiner; 13 years teaching and examining.

**▶ Live site:** https://cdw3lch.github.io/learning-design-samples/

How I introduce a new concept: start from what the learner already knows, make it something they can move and see, and let the formal result fall out of their own reasoning.

### What's inside

- **Interactive math explainers** — completing the square (geometric), why a negative times a negative is positive (pattern + proof), the derivative from first principles (secant → tangent), and a visual proof that the sum of the first *n* odd numbers is *n*². Self-contained, no libraries.
- **Web3 onboarding course** — a full, self-paced interactive course I designed end to end for adult beginners (scaffolded concept pages, reflections, and knowledge checks), developed through a design-based research study and exported as an IMS Common Cartridge. One module is also rebuilt in Genially to show authoring-tool fluency.
- **Instructional-design case study (San Diego Barracudas)** — a learner-research-driven onboarding design for a rugby league club: a new-player survey, a "negative transfer" insight for ex-rugby-union players, and a hybrid "Contrast + Stand-Alone" solution with a built-in measurement framework.
- **Built accessible** — keyboard-navigable, semantic headings, visible focus, text descriptions for every visual, live-updating explanations for screen readers, and reduced-motion support.
